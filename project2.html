Duolingo Connect


Duolingo Connect allows users to practice and apply their oral, reading, and writing skills by messaging other native speakers in real-time. Users will communicate in the language they're currently learning, and also have opportunities to receive feedback from other users.

Roles: Lead Interface & Experience Designer

Timeline: Summer 2023, May - August

Team: Jasper Ling & two other peers

Project Design Process > > > > >

1. Discover


To understand user painpoints with Duolingo, we began by assessing through user research interviews to understand what worked well, and what issues and frustrations they encountered during the app.

We conducted 3 interviews, asked 8 questions each, and observed all 3 when interacting with the app.

In summary, the major theme of the painpoints were that users felt that there was no opportunity for deeper learning, such that the content taught is not applicable to any real-life contexts, and lacked person-to-person interactions.

2. Define


After our user interviews, we used the data we gathered from our research to narrow down some ideas that could be turned into a new feature for Duolingo. After a brainstorm session and group discussion, we narrowed it down to 3 feature ideas.

Duolingo Chat: A way to apply knowledge learned in app, and engage in live conversations with players learning the opposite language to receive instant corrections & feedback.

Duolingo Versus: Applying knowledge competitively, by competing live with other same-level-players to answer realistic conversational based questions to test language comprehension.

Duolingo Library: Access deeper knowledge, by learning through popular literature, media and autio books, and by responding to comprehension questions.

3. Develop


After further discussions and analysis our feature ideas, we narrowed it down to Duolingo Chat, and began developing our wireframes, and idea execution. This involved creating wireframes, and then prototyping our mockups.

4. Deliver


After continuous discussions and feedback sessions, we polished up our mockups and concept of Duolingo Chat. These are just some of the screens of our major features, such as the onboarding process, the new chat interface with chat prompts and "How do I say", the new voice memos feature and of course, correcting the grammar usage of other peers.

Reflection


This was an incredibly fun project to work on, and really improved my Figma proficiency even further. I believe that this project would've benefited more if we had opportunity to test out our new feature designs on real people. And I also believe if we had a chance to interview more than 3 peers, we could've obtained more insightful data.
